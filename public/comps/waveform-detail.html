Macro view

The default arranger view. You see the overall energy envelope of the clip — useful for navigation and arrangement decisions, not for editing detail. Peaks compressed; RMS implied.

Close enough to edit specific transients. The envelope smoothing breaks down; we should start exposing individual sample peaks for precision work.

One vertical line per sample, sized to peak amplitude. Looks like a digital scope. Each transient is clearly individuated. Use when zoom ratio exceeds 1px per 2 samples.

Sample sticks behind a faint envelope outline. Best of both readings — you see overall shape and individual transients at the same time.
Zoom · 04

Sample-level

1 beat · 1 pixel per sample · 800px
Surgical precision. We're now drawing the actual oscillating signal — not an envelope. This is where dry/wet edits, sample-accurate trimming, and click repair happen.
Guitar A
1 beat
G — Continuous oscillation line
A single bright stroke connecting samples. The real signal shape. Like Pro Tools' sample-level edit view. Owner color at full saturation; no fill.
Guitar A
1 beat
H — Oscillation with soft glow halo
Same continuous stroke with a 3px gaussian halo at 18% opacity in owner color. Premium feel — like looking through bright phosphor on an oscilloscope.
Zoom · 05

Microscopic

≈ 2ms · 4–6 pixels per sample · 1000px
Individual sample points are visible. Used for clip-boundary surgery, click removal, dc offset fixing. Should never auto-zoom here; user must request it explicitly.
Guitar A
≈ 2 ms · ~96 samples
I — Connected sample dots
Each sample drawn as a 2px filled dot at its true vertical position, connected by a 1px line. A subtle horizontal zero-line at 6% opacity for reference. This is the truth of the audio at that resolution.

Recommendation

Adopt style D (RMS body + peak stroke) for the default arranger view. It reads at every zoom level from macro to detail edit without modification. At zoom levels deeper than 1 pixel per 4 samples, swap to style H (oscillation with glow halo). Add style I (sample dots) only as an explicit "sample-edit mode" — never automatically.
