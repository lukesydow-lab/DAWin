VU Physics · Attack / Decay

Attack rate: 32 /sec — the meter rises 32× per second from current toward target. At 60 fps that's ~53%/frame on a hot transient. You feel the kick the instant it hits.

Decay rate: 4 /sec — falls 4× slower than it rises. The meter glides down for ~250ms after a transient peak.

Why asymmetric: real audio peaks are short and the human ear's perception of loudness is sustained. Fast attack + slow decay matches both — you see every transient AND get a stable read on the level.

Peak Hold

Hold time: 700ms — a thin white bar marks the highest recent level.

Drop rate: 0.5 /sec after the hold — drifts down with visible deceleration so you can still see the recent peak even on quiet sections.

Why hold: a real LED meter peak indicator gives a moment to read the worst-case level without freezing the meter.

Per-Segment Glow

Lit segments have a 0 0 4px color-tinted glow. As a transient hits the topmost lit segment, the glow briefly intensifies to 0 0 8px for 80ms — a subtle "kiss" that emphasizes the new peak.

Color bands:

• Bottom 13 (0–65%): #1EC94A green
• Mid 4 (65–85%): #F5A623 amber
• Top 3 (85–100%): #E94560 red

Integrated Startup Performance

During the heartbeat sequence, the VU meters perform alongside the faders:

• Rise — meter blooms upward in sync with the fader spring (easeOutQuint)
• Hold — VU pulses gently at peak
• Fall — meter cascades downward with the fader (easeInOutQuart)

The peak-hold dots trail the journey, leaving visible "memory" of the heartbeat shape.
